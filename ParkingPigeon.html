Parking Pigeon: Application for Enhanced Localization in Multi-Story Parking Lots

Research Paper (2015)
Google Science Fair Submission
San Jose Mercury News Article
Google Slides

Awards
  • Regional Finalist (North and South Americas), Google Science Fair
  • Honorable Mention at the California State Science Fair
  • 1st Place in the Engineering Category at the Synopsys Science Fair
  • Congressional App Challenge Achievement Award from Congressman Mike Honda
  • Received Fair Manager’s Graphic Design Award for poster

Parking Pigeon is an Android smartphone application that determines the parking level of a car in a multi-story parking lot. Our application uses the smartphone’s built-in pressure sensors to measure the atmospheric pressure and calculate the altitude of the parked car. Parking Pigeon works in two parts. First, when parking the car, the app measures and record the atmospheric pressure of the parking level. It also queries the regional weather station to record the regional pressure. Second, at the time of locating the car, it queries a regional weather station to calculate any potential pressure drift due to changes in the weather, applies this pressure drift correction to the current pressure and then based on the pressure difference between the current pressure and the recorded pressure readings, it guides the user how high or low to go to find the parked car. After running various experiments with numerous smartphones on multiple days at various parking lots, where we compared the application’s predictions to the actual height, we found that the correlation was greater than 0.99. The application’s one meter error was found to be insignificant as most parking levels are 3-5 meters tall. With these results in mind, we knew Parking Pigeon was a success. We used pressure sensors, weather stations and web-query APIs in a novel method and met all our design criteria. After implementing further advancements to make the application user friendly and more automatic, we will release the Android Application onto the Play Store.
