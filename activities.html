USC

EurekaHacks

EurekaHacks is USC's first data science hackathon. Our 24-hour event is dedicated to educating students in the Southern California area about Data Science, fostering a much-demanded community in this field, and giving students an opportunity analyze data in order to understand and solve real-world problems. We expect to have around 400 attendees at our first event in February 2019. I am part of the logistics commitee, working on getting sponsors for the event, as well as handling key behind the scenes tasks. If you are interested in learning more, please contact me via email!

USC Interation Lab

I am part of the Interaction Lab, known for its socially assitive robots. You can learn about it here. I am working with Kate-Swift Spong on the EAGER project. Our goal is to create a socially assistive robot that increases a person's motivation for doing physical activities and exercise. Previous work done can be found on the Interaction Lab website. As a result of working on this project, I have become familiar with ROS.

HackSC Junior

HackSC Jr. is an one day hackathon that gives kids in grades 4-12 an opportunity to learn, explore, question, tinker and build through a one-day programming challenge. We emphasize diversity as well as the intersectional nature of tech and everyday life, demonstrating the various ways companies and people use computer science in their operations and encouraging attendees to continue learning outside of the event. Check out our website here.

As part of the Logistics/Operations committee, I help organize the event to ensure that it runs smoothly. At our event in March 2018, we had 300 kids participate, as well as 50 mentors teaching and encouraging them. It was a really fun experience and I can't wait for our 2019 event!

VRSC

I am part of the first incubator in USC's Virtual Reality club. We are taught the fundamentals of building a VR/AR/XR application. We also get the opportunity to work with others on our own project. My team is developing an augmented reality IOS application that allows users to display posters and other objects on their wall, giving them an idea of what it would like without having to actually hang them up.

Applied Statistics Club

I am an active member in USC's only data science club. I attend meetings to help grow my skillset and become more familiar with the tools that are used by data scientists on a daily basis.

USC ACM/IEEE/AAAI

I am actively involved in these 3 professional organizations. I enjoy meeting people who share the same passions for computer science and electrical engineering that I do

Monta Vista

Yearbook

As a head editor for Monta Vista's Yearbook during my senior year, I helped lead the overall vision of our Yearbook. I reviewed every page and trained a group of 25 students. I ensured that they were meeting deadlines and worked in a hierarchical structure, delegating tasks to section editors as necessary. In 2015-2016, I was the Senior Ads Editor, where I helped organize the purchases of Senior Ads in the Yearbook. I communicated with parents and Monta Vista staff members to ensure that they were happy with the pages that were being created, as most of them were surprises for the students.

Science Olympiad

As a freshman, I worked with upperclassmen to bring Science Olympiad to Monta Vista.

After winning six individual medals in my first two years of competing at the regional and state levels, I realized that team wins were more important to me. I mentored new members in my junior year, and decided to be an alternate to ensure that my mentees could compete. I was really happy when they won! I am currently the Vice President of Team Management, where I help mentor the teams and enhance overall group effort. I work to resolve any conflicts over events and have recently organized events to help with team bonding.

Science Olympiad is a team sport. We have emphasized collaboration and unity and have grown to new levels of performance every year. I am proud that I was one of the key founders of the Monta Vista Science Olympiad Club.
